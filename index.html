About

I am a mathematician working in combinatorics and graph theory, with a focus on extremal problems, Ramsey theory, and discrete optimization. I am currently a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Mount Holyoke College.

My work is motivated by a desire to understand structure in discrete systems, often through a mix of combinatorial, geometric, and algorithmic perspectives. I enjoy collaborating with students and colleagues, and I am especially interested in how combinatorial ideas interact with data science and AI.

Research

My research focuses on discrete geometry, discrete optimization, and extremal and structural problems in combinatorics and graph theory, particularly Ramsey-type questions and the study of geometric and algebraic graph invariants.

Research Interests

  • Extremal and structural graph theory
  • Ramsey theory and arithmetic Ramsey-type phenomena
  • Discrete geometry and polytopes
  • Combinatorial optimization and applications to data science

Selected Projects

  • Stem-symmetry and hang-symmetry in amoeba graphs.
    Structural properties of amoeba graphs and their relation to stem- and hang- symmetry, with connections to local and global embedding behavior.
  • Optimization tools for colorings with few monochromatic solutions.
    Using discrete optimization and computational methods to study colorings that avoid monochromatic solutions to linear equations.
